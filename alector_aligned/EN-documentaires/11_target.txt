The term Squirrel applies to animals that belong to the Sciuridae family. They are climbing rodents that have a tufted tail in plume.
There are 265 species of these mammals in the world.
These pleasant beasts are known for their effort to make provisions for the winter.
Squirrels are agile and fast.
They jump from branch to branch.
The alarm cry is a fast sound (couch, cuckold, cuckold...).
Several squirrels can help to rule out a predator. Squirrels then shout by shaking the tail.
The squirrel's tail is very useful.
It acts as a guide when the animal jumps from high places. The tail serves as a parasol when a clumsy squirrel falls. It serves as a cover during the winter.
The tail also allows a squirrel to indicate its plans to others.
Finally, the squirrel uses the tail to deceive its predators.
The fur of the squirrel is thicker and longer in winter.
Often the squirrel builds a leaf nest at the top of a tree or at a fork or on a branch near the trunk.
A floor of small branches forms the base of the nest, and leaves and braided stems form the tower.
The carcass is often made of braided leaves and stems.
Foam, grass and crushed bark line the interior. Squirrels also use fabric, paper, plants and bird feathers.
The duration of pregnancy of squirrels is about 40 days.
Squirrels are 1 or 2 litters per year, each with 3 to 6 pups.
Squirrels live about 4 to 6 years but in captivity they can live 15 to 20 years.
Moreover, the squirrel is the prey of many predators, including mink, marten, weasel, pekan, lynx, fox, coyote, owl, etc.
In the boreal forest, red squirrels feed on conifer seeds.
He also eats acorns, hazelnuts, seeds, buds, insects, bark, flowers, berries, mushrooms, bird eggs.
In the spring, the squirrel loves maple sap. Squirrels live all over the world.
